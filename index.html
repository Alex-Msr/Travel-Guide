Geology

Meteora, a geological marvel situated in central Greece, is a landscape shaped by the forces of nature over millions of years. The imposing sandstone pillars that define this region owe their existence to a complex geological history. Formed during the early Tertiary period, these colossal rock formations emerged from the seabed as a result of tectonic movements and subsequent uplifting. Erosion, both wind and water-driven, sculpted the rocks into the towering spires seen today.
