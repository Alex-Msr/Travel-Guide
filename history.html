History

Protected Heritage

Meteora, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a place where nature's geological wonders intertwine with a rich tapestry of history and spirituality. The name "Meteora" itself means "suspended in the air" or "in the heavens," perfectly describing the gravity-defying monasteries that have captured the imagination of visitors for centuries.

950AC 970AC

In the year 950 AC, a young monk named Elias found himself drawn to the remote and rugged landscapes surrounding the Pindus Mountains in Greece. Seeking solitude and a deeper connection with the divine, Elias embarked on a solitary pilgrimage, guided only by his unwavering faith and a vision of a sacred place. As he ventured deeper into the untouched wilderness, Elias stumbled upon valley dominated by towering rock formations. The cliffs seemed to reach for the heavens, creating an otherworldly atmosphere.

1000AC 1160AC

In the year 1000 AC, Meteora's first monastery, perched atop a towering rock pillar, stood as a testament to the unwavering devotion of its founders. The community of monks, inspired by the vision of Elias, had flourished, and the sacred haven became a beacon of spirituality in the Byzantine Empire.As news of the miraculous monastery spread, pilgrims from far and wide embarked on arduous journeys to experience the divine energy that seemed to emanate from the rocky spires.

1340AC 1350AC

In the year 1340 AC, Meteora, nestled in the heart of Thessaly, Greece, witnessed a period of profound transformation. The monasteries, perched high on their majestic rock pillars, had become bastions of Byzantine culture and spirituality. The echoes of prayers and the soft rustling of monks' robes blended harmoniously with the wind that swept through the towering cliffs.In 1350 AC, the inevitable clash between the Byzantines and the Ottoman Turks reached a critical juncture.

1920AC NOW

In the early 20th century, Meteora, a place where the divine met the earthly, underwent a new chapter in its rich history. The monasteries, with their ancient walls and timeless rituals, stood as witnesses to the unfolding changes in the world. In 1920, as Greece emerged from the turmoil of the First World War, Meteora faced a different kind of challenge. The monastic community, resilient as ever, navigated the shifting political landscape and societal changes that accompanied the birth of the modern Greek state.
